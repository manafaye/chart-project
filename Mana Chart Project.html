BLAPP App Helps Consumers Support Black-Owned Businesses

Census data shows Black-owned employer firms surged 57% from 2017–2022, yet they still receive fewer and smaller loans than other entrepreneurs.

By Mana Faye

Black-owned employer businesses in the United States grew from roughly 124,000 in 2017 to nearly 201,000 in 2023, a 57% increase, according to the U.S. Census Annual Business Survey. The jump reflects a wave of post-pandemic entrepreneurship and renewed consumer interest in supporting Black-owned brands.

However, despite this growth, data shows they face the lowest loan approval rates of any racial group. Even when approved, their loan approval rate has declined over the past five years.

"Black small business owners continue to disproportionately face challenges accessing affordable capital with fair terms, and on top of that, we’re often the target of predatory loans,” said Markevis Gideon, founder of NERDiT NOW in Wilmington, DE.

Some solutions are emerging from the community itself. BLAPP is an app that connects users to Black-owned businesses, helping them reach consumers directly. Founder Jon Laster says the platform is expanding rapidly. They hit 375,000 downloads as of mid-November 2025.

Advocates say apps like BLAPP are important because visibility can fill funding gaps, ultimately driving revenue, and stronger financials increase the chances of securing loans or an investment.

The trend is clear. Black owned businesses are rising, but systemic funding barriers are still present. Community-driven tools like BLAPP are helping close that gap, one customer at a time.
